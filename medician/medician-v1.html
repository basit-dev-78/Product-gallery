Proper Medicine Storage

Store medicines in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight and moisture. Keep them out of reach of children and check expiration dates regularly.

Medicine & Food Interactions

Some medicines interact with certain foods. For example, grapefruit can affect how some medications work. Always check with your pharmacist.

When to Consult a Doctor

Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about medicines and health information.

How should I store my medicines properly?

Most medicines should be stored in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight and moisture. Bathroom cabinets are often not ideal due to humidity. Always keep medicines in their original containers with lids tightly closed, and store them out of reach of children and pets. Some medicines may require refrigeration - check the label for specific storage instructions.

What should I do if I miss a dose of my medication?

If you miss a dose, take it as soon as you remember unless it's almost time for your next dose. In that case, skip the missed dose and continue with your regular schedule. Never take a double dose to make up for a missed one. For specific guidance, refer to the medication leaflet or consult your healthcare provider as instructions can vary by medication.

Are there common side effects I should watch for?

Common side effects vary by medication but may include nausea, dizziness, drowsiness, dry mouth, or headache. Most side effects are mild and temporary. However, seek immediate medical attention if you experience severe reactions like difficulty breathing, swelling of the face/lips/tongue, severe rash, or chest pain. Always read the patient information leaflet for specific side effects of your medication.

Can I take herbal supplements with my prescription medicine?

Some herbal supplements can interact with prescription medications, potentially reducing their effectiveness or causing adverse effects. For example, St. John's Wort can interact with many antidepressants and birth control pills. Always inform your healthcare provider about all supplements and over-the-counter medications you're taking to avoid potentially dangerous interactions.
